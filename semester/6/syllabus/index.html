CC 11 - Women’s Writing

Section A :

  • Emily Dickinson: “I cannot live with you”, “I’m wife; I’ve finished that”
  • Sylvia Plath: ‘Daddy’
  • Eunice De Souza: ‘Advice to Women’, ‘Bequest’
  • Jean Rhys: ‘Wide Sargasso Sea (ND)’

Section B :

  • Mary Wollstonecraft: A Vindication of the Rights of Woman (New York: Norton, 1988), Chapter 1 (pp. 11–19); Chapter 2 (pp. 19–38)(ND)
  • Ramabai Ranade: A Testimony of Our Inexhaustible Treasures" in Pandita Ramabai: Through Her Own Words: Selected Works, tr. Meera Kosambi (New Delhi: OUP, 2000), pp. 295–324 (ND)
  • Rassundari Debi: Amar Jiban (Excerpts in Women’s Writing in India, vol. 1, eds. Susie Tharu and K. Lalita, New Delhi: OUP, 1989, pp. 191–192) (ND)
  • Charlotte Perkins Gilman: “The Yellow Wallpaper”
  • Katherine Mansfield: “Bliss”
  • Mahasweta Devi: “Draupadi“ (Tr. Gayatri Spivak, Seagull Publishers, Kolkata)

CC 12 - British Literature (Early 20th Century)

Section A :

  • Virginia Woolf: ‘Mrs. Dalloway (ND)’
  • John Osborne: ‘Look Back in Anger’(Tr. Neeraj Malik)

Section B :

  • W.B. Yeats: “Leda and the Swan”, “The Second Coming”
  • T. S. Eliot: “The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock”, “The Hollow Men”
  • James Joyce: ‘A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man (ND)’

DSE 1 - Modern Indian Writing in English Translation

Section A :

  • Premchand: ‘The Shroud’, in Penguin Book of Classic Urdu Stories, ed. M. Assaduddin (New Delhi: Penguin/Viking, 2006) (ND)
  • Tagore: Streer Patra (tr. Supriya Chaudhuri, Oxford Book of Tagore’s Short Stories, New Delhi: Penguin Books, 2009) (ND)
  • Sharatchandra Chattopadhay: Mahesh (Drought and Other Stories, Sahitya Akadii edition) (ND)
  • Mahasweta Devi: ‘The Non-Veg Cow’, Tr. Paromita Banerjee (Seagull Publishers, Kolkata)(ND)
  • Rabindranath Tagore: Gitanjali(‘Where the mind is without fear’, ‘Leave thy chanting and singing and telling beads’, ‘Art thou abroad on this stormy night’, ‘Obstinate are the trammels, but my heart aches when I try to break them’)

Section B :

  • Tagore: Gora (tr. Sujit Mukerjee, Sahitya Akadii publishers, New Delhi) (ND)
  • M.K. Gandhi: Hind Swaraj (Tr. Anthony Parel. ‘Swaraj’, ‘Passive Resistance’, and ‘Education’)

DSE 2 - Partition Literature

Section A :

  • Amitav Ghosh: The Shadow Lines.
  • Khushwant Singh: Train to Pakistan (ND)

Section B :

  • Dibyendu Palit: ‘Alam's Own House’, tr. Sarika Chaudhuri in Bengal Partition Stories: An Unclosed Chapter, ed. Bashabi Fraser (London: Anthi Press, 2008) pp. 453–72.
  • Manik Bandhopadhya: ‘The Final Solution’, tr. Rani Ray, in Mapmaking: Partition Stories from Two Bengals, ed. Debjani Sengupta (New Delhi: Srishti, 2003) pp. 23–39.
  • Saadat Hasan Manto: “Toba Tek Sing”, in Black Margins: Manto, tr. M. Asaduddin (New Delhi: Katha, 2003) pp. 212–20.
  • Lalithambika Antharajanam: ‘A Leaf in the Storm’, tr. K. Narayana Chandran, in Stories about the Partition of India, ed. Alok Bhalla (New Delhi: Manohar, 2012).
  • Bapsi Sidhwa: Ice Candy Man (ND).
